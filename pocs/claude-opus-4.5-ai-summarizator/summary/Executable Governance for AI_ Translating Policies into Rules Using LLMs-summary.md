# Executable Governance for AI: Translating Policies into Rules Using LLMs

**arXiv ID**: 2512.04408
**PDF**: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2512.04408.pdf

---

Here's a structured summary of the paper titled "Executable Governance for AI: Translating Policies into Rules Using LLMs":

### 1. Overview
This paper explores the intersection of artificial intelligence (AI) governance and large language models (LLMs) by presenting a framework that translates policy guidelines into executable rules. The motivation lies in the increasing reliance on AI systems and the necessity for governance frameworks that ensure AI behavior aligns with ethical and regulatory standards. The authors aim to demonstrate how LLMs can facilitate the automation of governance by converting complex human-readable policies into structured, machine-understandable rules.

### 2. Key Contributions
- **Framework Development**: The paper introduces a systematic framework for translating policies into executable rules, utilizing the capabilities of LLMs.
- **Case Studies**: Through specific use cases, the paper illustrates how this framework can be applied in various governance scenarios, allowing for better compliance and oversight over AI systems.
- **Novelty in Automation**: The authors discuss the innovative aspect of automating the translation process, reducing reliance on manual intervention and thereby enhancing efficiency in policy enforcement.

### 3. Methodology
The methodology involves several key steps:
- **Data Collection**: Gathering existing policies regarding AI governance.
- **LLM Utilization**: Leveraging LLMs to interpret these policies and generate structured rules that encapsulate the intent and stipulations of the original documents.
- **Evaluation of Achievements**: The generated rules are assessed against predefined criteria to ensure they align with the original policy objectives.
- **Iterative Feedback Loop**: The authors employ a feedback mechanism to refine rule generation based on stakeholder input and compliance outcomes.

### 4. Results
The findings indicate that:
- The proposed framework effectively translates a diverse array of policies into coherent rules that can be executed by AI systems.
- There is a significant reduction in ambiguity and misinterpretation of policy provisions, leading to enhanced governance.
- Case studies demonstrate practical instances where the framework successfully implemented policy translation, resulting in measurable compliance improvements.

### 5. Implications
The implications of this research are profound:
- **Enhanced AI Compliance**: The ability to convert policies into executable formats may lead to better compliance with regulations, ultimately fostering trust in AI systems.
- **Scalability of Governance Efforts**: Organizations can efficiently scale their governance efforts, keeping pace with the rapid development of AI technologies.
- **Future of Policy Enforcement**: The framework establishes a groundwork for future AI governance efforts, potentially influencing regulatory bodies and policymakers to adopt similar approaches in their oversight mechanisms.

This paper positions itself as a significant contribution to the ongoing discourse on AI governance, addressing critical gaps in policy enforcement through innovative technological solutions.
