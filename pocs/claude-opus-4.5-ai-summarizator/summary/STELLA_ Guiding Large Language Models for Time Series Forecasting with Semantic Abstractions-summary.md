# STELLA: Guiding Large Language Models for Time Series Forecasting with Semantic Abstractions

**arXiv ID**: 2512.04871
**PDF**: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2512.04871.pdf

---

### 1. **Overview**
The paper titled "STELLA: Guiding Large Language Models for Time Series Forecasting with Semantic Abstractions" presents an innovative approach to enhance the performance of large language models (LLMs) specifically for the task of time series forecasting. The study introduces a system, named STELLA, which leverages semantic abstractions to improve forecasting accuracy and interpretability by integrating domain knowledge with sophisticated modeling techniques.

### 2. **Key Contributions**
The paper contributes to the field of time series forecasting through several key innovations:
- **Semantic Abstraction**: It presents a framework that utilizes semantic abstractions to better understand the underlying patterns in time series data, allowing LLMs to operate more effectively.
- **Guidance for LLMs**: The authors propose a methodology to guide the behavior of LLMs, making their predictions more relevant to the specific characteristics of time series data.
- **Performance Improvement**: STELLA is shown to outperform traditional forecasting methods and baseline LLMs in specific tasks, highlighting its effectiveness.

### 3. **Methodology**
The methodology of STELLA involves:
- **Data Preparation**: The approach starts with the preprocessing of time series data to extract meaningful features that can contribute to better semantic understanding.
- **Integration of Domain Knowledge**: The authors incorporate domain-specific knowledge into the model training process, improving its context sensitivity.
- **Training of LLMs**: Large language models are trained with guided prompts and structured inputs derived from time series data, enhancing their capability to learn relationships and patterns over time.
- **Evaluation Metrics**: The study employs various evaluation metrics to assess forecasting accuracy and model interpretability, ensuring a comprehensive analysis of STELLA's performance.

### 4. **Results**
The results demonstrate that STELLA significantly enhances the forecasting accuracy in comparison to standard LLMs and traditional methods. Key findings include:
- **Quantitative Performance**: STELLA achieves higher predictive performance metrics (e.g., lower mean absolute error, higher R² scores) across various datasets compared to baseline models.
- **Interpretability**: The use of semantic abstractions leads to improved interpretability of the model’s predictions, allowing stakeholders to better understand the reasoning behind forecasts.

### 5. **Implications**
The implications of this research are substantial for both academic and practical applications:
- **Improved Forecasting Capabilities**: Organizations relying on time series forecasting, such as those in finance, supply chain management, and healthcare, can benefit from the advanced capabilities provided by STELLA.
- **Integration of Semantic Knowledge**: By demonstrating the effective integration of semantic knowledge with LLMs, this work opens avenues for future research that further blends domain expertise with machine learning tactics.
- **Broader Applicability**: The techniques proposed could be adapted for other prediction tasks beyond time series forecasting, potentially influencing various fields where LLMs are relevant.

Overall, the paper presents a significant step forward in the understanding and application of large language models to complex forecasting problems, leveraging semantic insights to enhance predictive performance.
